KWON JIYONG

KNOWN TO HIS STAGE NAME: G-DRAGON

Here's a time line of Kwon Jiyong's life:

  • 1988 - Born on August 18th in Busan, South Korea
  • 1993 - As part of the Little Roo'Ra party, he started his career at the age of five.
  • 1994 - Cast as a dancer and actor in a popular morning show for kids called BboBboBboBboBbo
  • 1996 - He was trained in dance under SM Entertainment
  • 2001 - With the release of the album Flex, he became Korea's youngest rapper at 13 years of age. Signed a YG Entertainment deal
  • 2006 - Official debut with 5 other members as Big Bang group leader. Before its debut,
    Jang Hyun-seung was dropped, leaving 5 members still active today.
  • 2009 - Released first solo single Heartbreaker. Accused by Sony Music of plagiarising Flo Rida's Right 'Round
    and Oasis's She's Electric.
  • 2010 - The collaboration album of G Dragon and bandmate T.O.P. was released on Christmas.Eve and debuted at the top of the Gaon charts.
  • 2011 - Tested to be weed positive. No first offense was charged. Jiyong says that he took a cigarette from a fan in Japan, and he threw it away after realizing it was not a regular cigarette. All promotions had been halted and he was placed on a temporary break by YG. His first appearance following the scandal was a success at the MTV EMAs 2011
  • 2012 - Released One of a Kind which won him 2 "Best Hiphop / Rap Song of the Year" awards at the Korean Music Awards.
    The album placed first on the Billboard World Album Chart.
  • 2013 - As the first Korean solo artist to embark on a 4-dome tour in Japan and the second Korean solo artist to have a world tour, he started his first world tour. He later received 2 more awards at the World Music Awards.
  • 2014 - Collaborated with Taeyang on Good Boy, who reached number 1 on the "World Digital Songs" list of Billboard
  • 2015 - Under the name PEACEMINUSONE: Beyond the Stage, Jiyong started an exhibition featuring 200 works
    of art from local and international artists.
  • 2016 - G-Dragon spent the majority of 2015 and 2016 touring with his band for their third Korean album, performing for over 3.6 million fans in two years
  • 2017 - G-Dragon did a guesting to IU's concert
  • 2018 - G-Dragon began his two-year mandatory military service
  • 2020 - Discharged from his mandatory military service
  • 2021 - He's preparing for his greatest comeback as an artist
